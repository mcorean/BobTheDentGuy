How To Remove A Dent From Your Car

There are several ways you can fix a dent in a bumper. Some more complicated and expensive than others. One of them methods Bob uses is to heat the outside of the dented area or ding with a hot coil heating gun until the plastic is softer and mailable. With this alone some corners of the dent may pop back into place. Once it's ready to be worked with, he gets under the car and with a special tool wrapped in cloth, pushes against the dent from behind it while he continues applying heat to the outside of the bumper. Apply pressure slowly from behind as you very carefully reform the contours of the bumper.

Once about 90% of the dent has been popped back in, he takes a rag soaked in cold water to rub against the newly formed bumper plastic/rubber to harden it and maintain the shape. The rest of the edges of the dent will be shaped with a flat steel bar and heat from the heating gun. This shaping process evens out the rest of the dent to the point it looks like it was never there. Any paint chips that have flaked and chipped will be scrapped off and sanded down evenly with a DA Sander.

Between these two steps, the bumper begins to take it's final shape. The sanding process is very important to get right because the thickness of the adhesion promotor underneath needs to be uniform throughout for an even and smooth paint job.

Once sanded, you apply a mid-coat adhesion promotor made for plastics and spray on a few coats of a urethane primer and use a small amount of body filler to avoid cracking. This fills all the spaces in the bumper the sander couldn't get to and creates an even surface for new paint to be applied. After some primer and a god coat or two of matched paint any damage that was there before is now just a memory. Your bumper is as good as new without having to replace the entire thing.
